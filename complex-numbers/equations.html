Complex Numbers

- Quadratic Equations with Complex Roots

Remember that the discriminant (\(\Delta\)) of the quadratic formula is the term under the square root - \(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\).


Calculating the discriminant will tell us the nature of the roots.

  • If \(\Delta > 0\), two real roots exist.
  • If \(\Delta = 0\), one distinct root exists.
  • If \(\Delta < 0\), no real roots exist.

This means that the equation has complex roots if the discriminant has a negative value.


Example:

Solve the quadratic equation: \(z^2 + 3z + 4 = 0\).

To solve, we first assign the values: \(a=1,b=3,c=4\).

When calculating, the discriminant is less than zero: \[\eqalign{b^2 - 4ac &= 3^2 - 4 * 1 * 4 \\ &= 9-16 \\ &= -7}\]

Therefore, we know that the equation has complex roots. We can use the quadratic formula to find them: \[\eqalign{z &= \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \\ &= \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{3^2 - 4 * 1 * 4}}{2 * 1} \\ &= \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{9 - 16}}{2} \\ &= \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{-7}}{2} \\ &= \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{7 * -1}}{2} \\ &= \frac{-3 \pm (\sqrt{7} * \sqrt{-1})}{2}}\]

Remember that \(\sqrt{-1} = i\). Therefore: \[z = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{7}i}{2}\]

- Cubic Equations with Complex Roots

Similarly to quadratic equations, we can also get complex roots when solving cubic equations. They can either have three real roots, or one real root and a pair of complex conjugates.


Example:

To solve \(x^3 - 4x^2 + x + 26 = 0\), we can firstly use the factor theorem to find the real root. When substituting 1, -1, and 2 for x in the equations, the result is not zero, therefore we can discount these values.

We can now use long division with the factor to find: \[\frac{x^3 - 4x^2 + x + 26}{x+2} = x^2 - 6x + 13\]


Now we can use the quadratic formula to find the remaining pair of complex conjugates: \[\eqalign{x &= \frac{-(-6) \pm \sqrt{-6^2 - 4 * 1 * 13}}{2 * 1} \\ &= \frac{6 \pm \sqrt{32 -52}}{2} \\ &= \frac{6 \pm (\sqrt{16} * \sqrt{-1})}{2} \\ &= \frac{6 \pm 4i}{2} \\ &= 3 \pm 2i}\]

So, the three roots are \(x=-2\), \(3+2i\) and \(3-2i\).
