Differentiation

- Rates of Change

A rate of change is the ratio of change between a function, \(y\), and its independent variable, \(x\).

To find the rate of change we divide the change in the y-axis by the change in the x-axis: \[\text{rate of change} = \frac{\text{change in y}}{\text{change in x}}\]

This gives the formula: \[\text{rate of change} = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}\]


When the rate of change is constant, the graph is linear:

  • - a positive rate of change will give a positive slope from left to right.
  • - a negative rate of change will give a negative slope from left to right.
  • - zero rate of change will give a horizontal line.

When the rate of change is not constant, the graph will be non-linear:

  • - a positive rate of change will produce a positive graph.
  • - a negative rate of change will produce a negative graph.

Example:

• Find the estimate instantaneous rate of change using an equation: \(\text{Height} = -0.5t^2 + 4.6t\).

A plane trip from New Zealand to Antarctica takes approximately 8 hours. At 4 hours, the plane has an elevation of 10.4km.


To find the rate of change, we can find the gradient between time points 0.1 below and above our given point.

Let \(H = 3.9\) hours: \[\eqalign{H &= -0.5(3.9)^2 + 4.6(3.9) \\ &= 10.335}\]

Let \(H = 4.1\) hours: \[\eqalign{H &= -0.5(4.1)^2 + 4.6(4.1) \\ &= 10.455}\]

Then we substitute the answers into the rate of change formula: \[\eqalign{\text{Rate of change} &= \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \\ &= \frac{10.455 - 10.335}{4.1 - 3.9} \\ &= 0.6}\] Therefore, the estimated instantaneous rate of change for elevation of the plane after 4 hours is 0.6km/h.
